SECTION 1 — SYSTEM OVERVIEW

◆ 1.1 Purpose & Scope

This rulebook is the foundational intelligence document for the AI-powered trading system. Every decision made by the system — from market analysis to trade execution to position management — must derive exclusively from the rules, definitions, and examples contained within this document. No deviation is permitted.

The system operates across three modules:

-   Module A — Market Intelligence Engine: macro/fundamental analysis + technical analysis

-   Module B — Trade Execution Engine: order placement and risk calculation

-   Module C — Trade Management Engine: position management from entry to final exit

◆ 1.2 Analytical Frameworks

The system reasons exclusively from the intersection of three frameworks:

◆ 1.3 Core Philosophy

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  📌 The AI executes what the rules dictate. It does not improvise, extrapolate, or override. If a situation is not covered by this rulebook, the system outputs NO SETUP and waits for the next analysis cycle.

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

-   Every trade must have a clear, documentable reason grounded in this rulebook

◆ 2.5 Style-Specific Rules

Scalping Mode

-   Entry and exit targets are derived from 15M and 1H structure — 4H provides immediate context, 1D provides directional bias only

-   Minimum R:R in scalping mode: 1:2 (lower than other styles due to tighter structure)

-   No runners — all position must be fully closed within the same session

-   Maximum trade duration: 2 hours from entry. If TP not hit within 2 hours — close at market

-   News lockout window extended to 45 minutes in scalping mode due to higher sensitivity to volatility spikes

-   Spread threshold is tighter — reject if spread exceeds 1.5x normal average (vs 2x in other styles)

Intraday Mode

-   1W and 1D used for directional context only. Entry and TP targets built from 4H and 1H structure.

-   All trades evaluated at 16:30 UTC — close protocol applies (see Section 9.3)

-   Overnight carry permitted only if: in profit beyond TP1 + SL at break-even + no high-impact news before next London open. Dashboard toggle required.

-   No weekend carry under any circumstances

-   Minimum R:R: 1:3

Swing Mode

-   Full top-down analysis from 1W to 1H. All timeframes carry equal analytical weight.

-   Trades can hold overnight and across multiple sessions

-   Weekend carry permitted only if: SL is at break-even or better + no extreme geopolitical or macro risk identified + overnight carry dashboard toggle is ON

-   Friday 16:00 UTC: evaluate all open swing trades. If SL not at BE — close. If SL at BE or better — may hold over weekend.

-   Minimum R:R: 1:3. Target R:R: 1:5+

-   Trailing switches from 1H to 4H after TP1 is hit

Positional Mode

-   Analysis anchored to Monthly and Weekly charts for direction. Daily used for entry context.

-   Entries are wider — OBs and SnD zones on 1D and 1W only. No 4H entries in positional mode.

-   Weekend carry is standard — this is expected and planned for in SL placement

-   SL placement accounts for weekly ranges — wider stops, smaller lot sizes, same % risk

-   No EOD closure protocol — positional trades run until invalidation or TP is reached

-   Minimum R:R: 1:5. Target R:R: 1:10+

-   COT and macro fundamentals carry highest weight in positional mode — technical structure is secondary

◆ 2.6 Day-Based Restrictions (All Styles)

-   Monday: No new trade entries before London Open (07:00 UTC) — low institutional participation and gap risk from weekend

-   Friday: In Scalping and Intraday modes — no new entries after 12:00 UTC. In Swing mode — no new entries after 14:00 UTC. Positional mode — no restriction but positions should already be established.

-   Weekend: Scalping and Intraday — all positions closed by Friday 16:00 UTC. Swing — only positions with SL at BE or better may carry. Positional — standard hold.

SECTION 3 — TIMEFRAME HIERARCHY & ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

◆ 3.1 Top-Down Analysis Framework

Analysis must always flow top-down. A lower timeframe signal is only valid when it aligns with the higher timeframe context. Conflict between timeframes = NO SETUP.

  ------------- ------------------------ -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Timeframe     Role                     What To Identify

  1W (Weekly)   Macro Structure          Primary trend direction, major S&D zones, Wyckoff phase, HTF liquidity pools

  1D (Daily)    Intermediate Structure   Swing highs/lows, BOS/ChoCH confirmation, key daily OBs, FVGs, intermediate S&D zones

  4H            Entry Context            Order blocks, FVGs, liquidity sweeps, ChoCH for entry signal, precise zone identification

  1H            Entry Timing             Final entry confirmation, micro-structure alignment, limit order placement precision
  ------------- ------------------------ -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

◆ 3.2 Timeframe Alignment Rule

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  ⚠ A trade is only valid when 1W, 1D, and 4H all agree on direction. If any one timeframe contradicts the thesis — the setup is rejected regardless of how strong the others appear.

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Alignment criteria by direction:

-   LONG: 1W bullish structure + 1D BOS to upside confirmed + 4H demand zone intact + 4H ChoCH bullish

-   SHORT: 1W bearish structure + 1D BOS to downside confirmed + 4H supply zone intact + 4H ChoCH bearish

  ------------------------------------ -------------------------------------- ----------------------------------------- -------------

◆ 4.2 COT (Commitments of Traders) Rules

COT data is released every Friday by the CFTC and covers positioning as of the prior Tuesday. The system pulls and interprets this data weekly.

-   Non-Commercial (Speculative) positioning is the primary signal — these are the large institutions and hedge funds

-   BULLISH signal: Net long positioning increasing week-over-week + not yet at extreme

-   BEARISH signal: Net short positioning increasing week-over-week + not yet at extreme

-   CAUTION — Extreme positioning: When specs are at multi-year extreme longs or shorts, a reversal is likely. Do not enter in the direction of the extreme — treat as reversal warning

-   COT alone is never sufficient. It must align with technical structure to be actionable

◆ 4.3 High-Impact News Event Rules

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  ⚠ No new trade entries within 30 minutes BEFORE or 30 minutes AFTER any high-impact scheduled news event (Red folder events on Forex Factory). Violations are automatic rejection.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

-   High-impact events include: FOMC, ECB/BOE/BOJ rate decisions, NFP, CPI, GDP

-   If a trade is open when news hits: do not close unless SL is triggered — let the rules handle it

-   Post-news spike wicks are frequently liquidity sweeps — do not chase them

-   System monitors economic calendar on every analysis cycle and flags upcoming events

SECTION 5 — TECHNICAL ANALYSIS RULES

◆ 5.1 Market Structure Rules

Break of Structure (BOS)

-   A BOS occurs when price closes a candle BEYOND a previous swing high (bullish BOS) or swing low (bearish BOS)

-   BOS must be a full candle close — wicks do not count as BOS

-   Bullish BOS confirms continuation of uptrend — look for demand zones to form

-   Bearish BOS confirms continuation of downtrend — look for supply zones to form

Change of Character (ChoCH)

-   ChoCH is the first BOS in the OPPOSITE direction of the prevailing trend — the initial signal that trend may be reversing

-   ChoCH on 4H within a 1D bullish trend = entry context for longs (shows pullback exhaustion)

-   ChoCH alone is not an entry. It identifies where to look for the entry zone, not the entry itself

-   ChoCH must be followed by a retracement back into the identified OB/FVG/SnD zone before entry is considered

◆ 5.2 Smart Money Concepts (SMC) — Definitions & Rules

Order Blocks (OB)

-   Definition: The last opposing candle before a significant impulsive move. For a bullish OB — the last bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse. For a bearish OB — the last bullish candle before a strong bearish impulse.

-   VALID OB criteria: (1) Strong impulse departure from the OB, (2) The impulse results in a BOS, (3) OB has not been previously mitigated (price returning to the zone and trading through it)

-   INVALID OB: Any OB where price has already traded back through it and closed beyond it

-   OB entry zone: High to low of the OB candle (or refined to 50% of the OB for higher probability)

-   Refined OB (Optimal Trade Entry — OTE): 62%–79% retracement into the OB is the highest probability entry area

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

-   Definition: A 3-candle formation where the high of candle 1 does not overlap with the low of candle 3, leaving an imbalance (gap) in candle 2

-   Bullish FVG: Candle 3 low is above candle 1 high — price will frequently return to fill this gap

-   Bearish FVG: Candle 3 high is below candle 1 low

-   FVGs are strongest when they form within or adjacent to an OB on the same timeframe

-   FVG is considered mitigated when price trades back into the 50% level of the gap

Liquidity Sweeps

-   Definition: Price temporarily exceeds a key swing high or low (taking out stop losses resting there) before reversing sharply

-   Liquidity sweep above a swing high followed by a bearish reversal = BEARISH signal (distribution)

-   Liquidity sweep below a swing low followed by a bullish reversal = BULLISH signal (accumulation/spring)

-   A sweep is only valid if price REJECTS the level with a strong wick or engulfing candle and closes back inside the range

-   Sweeps into 4H OBs or 1D FVGs are the highest-quality entry scenarios in the entire system

Breaker Blocks

-   Definition: A previously valid OB that has been mitigated (price traded through it). When price returns to this level from the other side, it acts as resistance (former support) or support (former resistance)

-   Breaker blocks confirm that structure has shifted and provide re-entry opportunities in the new direction

-   Breaker blocks carry lower weight than fresh OBs — require additional confluence to qualify as entry

◆ 5.3 Supply & Demand Zone Rules

Zone Identification

-   A valid Supply zone originates from a sharp bearish impulse that departed so strongly it left a gap or imbalance. The zone is the consolidation or base candle(s) immediately before the impulse

-   A valid Demand zone originates from a sharp bullish impulse under the same criteria

-   The origin candle(s) must show strong displacement — indecisive candles do not qualify

Zone Validity Rules

-   FRESH ZONES ONLY: A zone that has never been tested since its creation is the highest grade (Grade A)

-   PARTIALLY TESTED: A zone where price touched the outer edge but did not close within the zone body — Grade B. Still tradeable if higher TF structure supports it

-   MITIGATED ZONE: A zone where price has closed a candle within or through the zone body — INVALID. Do not trade mitigated zones

-   Zone strength ranking: 1W zone > 1D zone > 4H zone. Higher timeframe zones take precedence when there is a conflict

Zone Grading Criteria

  ---------------------------- -------------------------- ------------------- ----------------
  Criteria                     Grade A                    Grade B             Invalid

  Prior mitigation             Never tested               Edge touch only     Body mitigated

  Impulse strength from zone   Strong, breaks structure   Moderate impulse    Weak departure

  Timeframe of zone            1D or 1W                   4H                  Below 4H only

  HTF structure alignment      Full alignment             Partial alignment   No alignment
  ---------------------------- -------------------------- ------------------- ----------------

◆ 5.4 Wyckoff Phase Identification Rules

  ---------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------
  Phase / Event          Description                                                                         System Action

  Accumulation           Range-bound after downtrend. Institutions absorbing supply. Volume diminishing.     Prepare for long setups. Look for Spring event.

  Spring                 Final sweep below accumulation range low. Shakeout of weak longs. Sharp reversal.   Highest priority LONG entry. Confirms accumulation complete.

  Markup (Uptrend)       Price rising out of accumulation with increasing volume. Higher highs and lows.     Long continuation setups on LPS retracements to demand.

  Distribution           Range-bound after uptrend. Institutions offloading supply. Volume irregular.        Prepare for short setups. Look for Upthrust event.

  Upthrust (UTAD)        Final sweep above distribution range high. Traps late longs. Sharp reversal.        Highest priority SHORT entry. Confirms distribution complete.

  Markdown (Downtrend)   Price falling out of distribution. Lower highs and lower lows.                      Short continuation setups on LPSY retracements to supply.
  ---------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — RISK MANAGEMENT RULES

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  ⚠ Risk management rules are hardcoded. The AI cannot override, circumvent, or reinterpret them under any circumstances. They are the final defense of capital.

  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

◆ 7.1 Position Sizing Rules

-   Risk per trade: 1% of current account balance on A/A+ grade setups

-   Risk per trade: 0.5% of current account balance on B grade setups

-   Lot size formula: (Account Balance × Risk %) ÷ (Stop Loss in pips × Pip Value)

-   Lot size is calculated fresh on every trade based on current balance — never static

-   Fractional lots are used to achieve precise risk amounts

◆ 7.2 Portfolio Risk Controls

  ----------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Rule                                Parameter

  Maximum concurrent open trades      3 trades maximum at any time

  Correlated pair exposure            Max 1 trade per correlated group (e.g. EURUSD + GBPUSD = same USD group). Take the strongest setup only.

  Maximum daily loss limit            3% of account. If hit — execution locked until next trading day.

  Maximum weekly drawdown             5% of account. If hit — full system pause until Monday.

  Maximum monthly drawdown            10% of account. If hit — system pauses, full review required before resumption.

  Weekend exposure                    No trades to carry over the weekend with SL more than 50 pips from entry unless exceptional thesis.
  ----------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

◆ 7.3 Minimum Reward-to-Risk Requirements

  ----------------- ----------------- ----------------- -----------------------------------------------
  Trading Style     Minimum R:R       Target R:R        Note

  Scalping          1:2               1:3               Tighter structure, tighter targets

  Intraday          1:3               1:5               Same-day resolution limits range

SECTION 8 — ENTRY EXECUTION RULES

◆ 8.1 Execution Mode — Dashboard Selection

The system supports two execution modes. Both run the identical analysis pipeline — full Module A cycle, all confirmation rules, all confluence requirements, all RAG validation. The mode controls only the execution mechanism after the setup is fully validated.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  📌 Same intelligence. Same rules. Same confirmations. The mode only changes HOW the order is triggered — not WHAT is analyzed before it.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ---------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                   LIMIT MODE                                                                                                                  INSTANT MODE

  Mechanism        Limit order placed at broker immediately after analysis completes. Broker fills when price naturally arrives at the zone.   Module C Go goroutine watches price tick by tick. Market order fires the moment price touches the confirmed entry level.

  Order Type       Limit order — exact price guaranteed, zero slippage by design                                                               Market order — at-touch price, minimal slippage

  Best For         Clean zone retracements where price is expected to arrive calmly                                                            Sweep entries, fast-moving price, zones where pre-placing risks a bad fill on overshoot

  Pending Expiry   TTL configurable per style. Auto-cancelled if structure breaks before fill.                                                 Goroutine disarms on structure break or overshoot beyond tolerance.
  ---------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

◆ 8.2 Entry Levels — Both Modes

-   Entry price for Limit Mode: placed at OTE (62–79% of OB) or top/bottom of confirmed SnD zone

-   Entry level for Instant Mode: goroutine watch level set to same OTE or zone level — fires on touch

-   Both modes use the identical entry level derived from the same Module A analysis output

-   SL is placed structurally beyond the invalidation level for both modes — never a fixed pip value

-   SL is never placed on a round number — minimum 3 pip offset to avoid stop hunts

◆ 8.3 Take Profit Structure — Both Modes

-   SL placement: Structurally beyond the level that invalidates the trade thesis

-   For OB entries: SL placed below/above the OB low/high + spread buffer (3–5 pips)

-   For SnD zone entries: SL placed beyond the full zone with a buffer

-   For liquidity sweep entries: SL placed beyond the sweep wick extreme

-   SL is NEVER placed on a round number — offset by at least 3 pips to avoid stop hunts

-   SL must be structural, not a fixed pip value — the market dictates SL distance, risk % dictates lot size

  -------------- ----------------- ------------------------------------------------------------ -----------------------
  Target         Position Closed   Level                                                        Post-TP Action

  TP1            40%               Nearest liquidity pool / minor resistance / previous swing   Move SL to break-even

  TP2            30%               Major S&D zone / HTF key level / 1D structure target         Trail SL to TP1 level

  TP3 (Runner)   30%               1W key level / final macro target / let trail do the work    Trail on 4H structure
  -------------- ----------------- ------------------------------------------------------------ -----------------------

SECTION 9 — TRADE MANAGEMENT RULES

◆ 9.1 Break-Even Rules

-   Break-even trigger fires when price reaches TP1 level — SL immediately moves to entry + spread buffer (2–3 pips)

-   Scalping exception: BE triggers when price moves 60% toward TP1 on the 15M chart — tighter due to shorter holding time

-   Intraday exception: If TP1 not reached within 3 hours — tighten SL to 50% of original risk as time-based risk reduction

-   Break-even is one-directional — SL only moves in profit direction, never pulled back

◆ 9.2 Trailing Stop Rules — Style Adaptive

  ----------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------
  Trading Style     Trail Method                                                                      Recalculation Frequency

  Scalping          Trail to most recent 15M swing high/low                                           Every 15M candle close

  Intraday          Trail to most recent 1H swing high/low. Switch to 4H after London/NY overlap.     Every 1H candle close

  Swing             Trail to most recent 4H swing high/low after TP1. Switch to 1D swing after TP2.   Every 4H candle close

  Positional        Trail to most recent 1D swing high/low after TP1. Switch to 1W swing after TP2.   Every daily close
  ----------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------

◆ 9.3 End-of-Period Management — Style Specific

Scalping — End of Session Protocol

-   All scalp trades must be closed before session end regardless of status

-   Maximum trade duration: 2 hours from entry — close at market if exceeded

-   No carry to next session under any circumstances

Intraday — End of Day Protocol (16:30 UTC)

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  📌 All intraday trades are evaluated at 16:30 UTC daily. System executes automatically.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------
  Trade Status at 16:30 UTC                                                        System Action

  In profit — TP1 hit — SL at break-even                                           Close remaining at market. Log WIN.

  In profit — TP1 not yet hit                                                      Close at market. Log partial WIN.

  At break-even                                                                    Close at market. Log BREAK-EVEN.

  In loss — SL not hit                                                             Close at market. Log LOSS. No overnight hope-holding.

  Overnight carry exception (dashboard toggle ON + SL at BE + no overnight news)   Hold. Re-evaluate at next London open.
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------

Swing — Weekend Protocol (Friday 16:00 UTC)

-   SL at break-even or better + dashboard overnight toggle ON → may hold over weekend

-   SL still in original loss territory → close at market regardless of unrealised profit

-   Re-evaluate all swing positions at Monday 07:00 UTC London open

Positional — No EOD Protocol

-   No end-of-day forced closure — positional trades run on their own structure

-   Weekly review every Friday 15:00 UTC — assess whether thesis is still intact

-   If 1W candle close invalidates the thesis → close Monday open

◆ 11.2 Key Performance Metrics to Track

-   Overall win rate (%)

-   Win rate by pair — identify strongest performing instruments

-   Win rate by setup type — OB vs FVG vs SnD vs Wyckoff events

-   Average R multiple per trade

-   Best performing session — London vs NY vs Overlap

-   Expectancy: (Win Rate × Avg Win R) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss R)

-   Module A confluence score vs actual outcome — calibration metric

-   Monthly drawdown track

-   Consecutive wins / losses — behavioral pattern detection

-   Monthly: Full performance report generated. Expectancy recalculated. Confluence score calibration reviewed.

-   Quarterly: Deep review of RAG knowledge base — are the rules still producing edge? Any rule needs updating based on real outcomes?

◆ 12.2 Hallucination Prevention Protocol

-   All technical analysis decisions are retrieved from the RAG knowledge base — not generated from the model's general training

-   If a market scenario is not covered by an existing rule or annotated example in the knowledge base — the system outputs NO SETUP

-   The system cites the specific rule or example from the knowledge base that supports every decision made

-   Conflicting signals across timeframes are never resolved by assumption — they result in NO SETUP

-   The system never fabricates confluence — every factor in the confluence score must be verifiably present in the data
